How to Calculate Fuel Cost Per Person

To calculate fuel cost per person manually, you need to consider a few key factors:

  • Total distance of the trip
  • Vehicle fuel efficiency (km/l or mpg)
  • Fuel price per liter or gallon
  • Number of people sharing the cost

Basic formula:

  • Total Fuel Cost = Distance ÷ Mileage × Fuel Price
  • Cost Per Person = Total Fuel Cost ÷ Number of People

While this seems simple, mistakes can easily happen — especially when dealing with long trips or multiple stops.

Example: Fuel Cost Per Person Calculation

Let’s understand with a real-world example:

  • Trip distance: 300 km
  • Car mileage: 15 km/l
  • Fuel price: ₹100/litre
  • Number of people: 4

Step 1: Fuel needed = 300 ÷ 15 = 20 litres

Step 2: Total fuel cost = 20 × 100 = ₹2000

Step 3: Cost per person = ₹2000 ÷ 4 = ₹500

How to Calculate Gas Cost Per Mile

Another common method is calculating gas cost per mile or kilometer.

  • Gas cost per mile = Fuel price ÷ Mileage
  • Trip fuel cost = Cost per mile × Total distance

This method is useful when estimating fuel expenses for long-distance travel.

How to Calculate Gas Cost for a Trip (Round Trip vs One Way)

One of the biggest mistakes people make is forgetting to include the return journey.

  • One-way trip: Calculate only the distance to the destination
  • Round trip: Double the distance for accurate cost

For example, a 200 km trip actually becomes 400 km when returning.

Always include the full journey to avoid underestimating fuel costs.
