Gas Cost Formula for Road Trips

Fuel cost for a road trip can be estimated using a simple formula:

Total Fuel Cost = (Trip Miles ÷ Vehicle MPG) × Fuel Price

For example, if your trip distance is 350 miles, your vehicle gets 28 miles per gallon, and gas costs $3.70 per gallon, the calculation would be:

  • Fuel required = 350 ÷ 28 = 12.5 gallons
  • Total fuel cost = 12.5 × $3.70 = $46.25

If four passengers are sharing the trip, each person would contribute approximately $11.56.

Should the Driver Pay for Gas on a Road Trip?

One of the most common questions on group trips is whether the driver should pay the same amount as passengers for fuel.

There is no universal rule, but most groups follow one of these common approaches:

  • Equal split – Everyone, including the driver, pays the same share.
  • Driver discount – Passengers cover slightly more since the driver provides the vehicle.
  • Passenger split – Only passengers split the fuel cost while the driver pays nothing.

The best method depends on your group and trip length. A fuel cost split calculator helps estimate total fuel expenses so everyone understands the cost before the trip begins.
