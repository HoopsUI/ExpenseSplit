To split a restaurant bill with tax and tip:

  1. Add total bill (food + tax + tip)
  2. Calculate each person’s share based on what they ordered
  3. Divide tax proportionally based on individual spending
  4. Split tip either equally or based on spending

Step-by-Step: How to Calculate Tax & Tip Split

1. Calculate the Total Bill

Start by adding the food total, tax, and tip:

  • Food Total: ₹2,000
  • Tax (10%): ₹200
  • Tip (10%): ₹200
  • Total Bill: ₹2,400

2. Decide the Splitting Method

You can split the bill in two main ways:

  • Equal Split → Everyone pays the same amount
  • Item-Based Split → Each person pays for what they ordered

3. Split Tax Proportionally

Tax should be divided based on how much each person spent. For example:

  • Person A ordered ₹1,000 → pays more tax
  • Person B ordered ₹500 → pays less tax

This ensures fairness instead of splitting tax equally.

4. Split Tip Fairly

Tip is usually calculated as a percentage of the total bill. You can:

  • Split tip equally (simple method)
  • Split tip based on individual spending (fair method)

Formula to Calculate Tax & Tip Split

If you want to calculate everything manually, use this formula:

  • Individual Share = Food Cost + (Tax × Your % of total) + (Tip × Your % of total)

Example:

  • Total food = ₹2,000
  • Your order = ₹1,000 (50%)
  • Tax = ₹200 → Your share = ₹100
  • Tip = ₹200 → Your share = ₹100
  • Total you pay = ₹1,200

This method ensures accurate and fair bill splitting, especially when orders are uneven.

Edge Cases When Splitting Tax & Tip
