Best Ways to Split Gas Costs on a Road Trip

1. Equal Split Per Person

The simplest method is dividing total fuel cost equally among all passengers.

Example: ₹4,000 fuel cost ÷ 4 people = ₹1,000 each.

2. Split Based on Distance Travelled

If passengers join or leave at different points, splitting based on distance is more fair.

3. Split Based on Who Paid

When different people pay for fuel during the trip, a calculator can determine who owes whom at the end.

4. Carpool Cost Sharing

In carpools, fuel cost is often split based on usage, distance, or number of trips.

Real Example: Splitting Gas Costs on a Road Trip

Let’s say you and your friends are going on a road trip:

  • Total distance: 500 km
  • Car mileage: 20 km/l
  • Fuel price: ₹95/l
  • People in car: 5

Step-by-step calculation:

  • Fuel required = 500 ÷ 20 = 25 litres
  • Total fuel cost = 25 × 95 = ₹2,375
  • Cost per person = ₹2,375 ÷ 5 = ₹475

This is a simple case, but in real trips:

  • Fuel is bought multiple times
  • People may join or leave
  • Distances may vary

That’s why using a calculator is much more practical than manual calculations.

Fuel Cost Per Person Formula

If you want to calculate gas cost manually, you can use this simple formula:

  • Total Distance (km)
  • Vehicle Mileage (km per litre)
  • Fuel Price per litre

Formula:

Total Fuel Cost = (Distance ÷ Mileage) × Fuel Price

Cost Per Person = Total Fuel Cost ÷ Number of People

Example:

  • Distance = 300 km
  • Mileage = 15 km/l
  • Fuel price = ₹100/l

Fuel used = 300 ÷ 15 = 20 litres Total cost = 20 × 100 = ₹2,000 Split between 4 people = ₹500 per person

Fuel Cost Calculator Per KM and Per Mile (Global Guide)

If you want to calculate fuel cost per distance, you can use either kilometers (km) or miles depending on your location.

Fuel Cost Per KM Formula

  • Fuel cost per km = Fuel price per litre ÷ Mileage (km per litre)

Example:

  • Fuel price = ₹100/litre
  • Mileage = 20 km/litre

Cost per km = ₹100 ÷ 20 = ₹5 per km

Fuel Cost Per Mile Formula

  • Fuel cost per mile = Fuel price per gallon ÷ Mileage (miles per gallon)

Example:

  • Fuel price = $4/gallon
  • Mileage = 25 miles/gallon

Cost per mile = $4 ÷ 25 = $0.16 per mile
