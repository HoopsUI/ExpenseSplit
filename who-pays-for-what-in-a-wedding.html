Traditional Wedding Cost Breakdown

Who Pays for a Wedding in India?

In Indian weddings, traditions can vary widely based on culture, region, and family expectations. Historically, the bride’s family covered most wedding expenses, including venue, catering, and decorations.

However, modern Indian weddings are shifting toward shared responsibility:

  • Both families contribute financially
  • The couple may fund part of the wedding
  • Expenses are split based on events (haldi, mehndi, reception)

Because Indian weddings often include multiple events and higher costs, planning a clear wedding budget breakdown is essential.

Quick Answer: In modern weddings, both families and the couple usually share expenses based on budget, preferences, and financial capacity.

Real-Life Wedding Cost Scenarios (What Most Couples Do)

Every wedding is different, and cost splitting often depends on family dynamics, culture, and financial situations. Here are some common real-life scenarios to help you decide what works best for you:

1. Both Families Contribute Equally

This is one of the most common modern approaches. Both families agree on a budget and split it 50/50.

2. Couple Pays for Majority

Many modern couples prefer financial independence and cover most wedding expenses themselves, with optional contributions from parents.

3. One Family Pays More

If one family has a higher financial capacity, they may choose to contribute more to reduce stress on the other side.

4. Destination Wedding Split

In destination weddings, guests often pay for their travel and accommodation, while the couple covers the ceremony and main events.
