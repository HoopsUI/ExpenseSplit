How to Create a Wedding Budget (Step-by-Step Guide)

If you're starting from scratch, the best way to plan your wedding finances is to break the process into simple steps. This ensures you stay within budget and avoid unexpected costs.

  1. Set your total budget – Decide how much you can realistically spend.
  2. List all expense categories – Include venue, catering, attire, photography, and extras.
  3. Prioritize what matters most – Allocate more budget to what’s important (venue, food, etc.).
  4. Estimate costs for each category – Use average percentages as a guide.
  5. Track every expense – Record who paid for what.
  6. Adjust as needed – Rebalance your budget when costs change.

How to Split Wedding Costs Fairly

Splitting wedding expenses can be complicated, especially when multiple families or contributors are involved.

1. Equal Split Between Partners

The simplest approach is dividing total costs equally between the couple.

2. Split by Category

Each family pays for specific parts of the wedding (venue, catering, etc.).

3. Contribution-Based Split

Each person contributes based on their financial capacity.

4. Track & Settle Later

Track all expenses and calculate final balances at the end.
